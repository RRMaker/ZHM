Our Innovation

Zebrafish Hemostasis Mapper (ZHM) is a computer-vision-based system to automatically trace zebrafish in zebrafish bleeding assays, enabling faster and more objective analysis. It is designed to be used for academic research laboratories conducting hemostasis, thrombosis, and blood-related research using zebrafish models, as well as pharmaceutical and biotech companies.

Zebrafish are widely used for studying hemostasis, thrombosis, and vascular biology due to their genetic similarity to humans. One of the common and most necessary steps is tracing an outline around the entire zebrafish after cutting it and letting it bleed out. Due to the limitations of current imaging software, this is extremely time consuming, as researchers and companies must manually trace the outline with a computer mouse.

In addition, this leads to minor inconsistencies in the outlining, which can heavily affect research and company operations. Due to current software limitations and heavy consequences, we decided to make ZHM, an application which solves these problems and allows research laboratories and companies to dramatically increase there efficiency.

ZHM works by analyzing images of zebrafish assays and automatically identifying the outline of the fish based on visual differences between the fish and the background. Instead of requiring a researcher to manually trace the outline using a mouse, the system detects color, brightness, and shape patterns in the image to determine where the zebrafish begins and ends. Once the outline is identified, the system traces it consistently and outputs a clean boundary that can be used for measurement and comparison. This process produces faster, more consistent results while removing much of the subjectivity and variability that comes with manual tracing.
